# Brent Crude Oil Market State – Past 30 Days Analysis (Dec 24, 2025 – Jan 23, 2026)

## 1. OPEC+ Production Policy
In early January 2026, OPEC+ announced the suspension of its scheduled production increases for Q1 2026, reflecting clear concerns about oversupply and ongoing sanctions on major producers such as Russia. This pause is meant to stabilize Brent prices, following recent inventory builds despite existing disruptions ([AP News](https://apnews.com/article/790431afcabf60aad64075c4979ab606?utm_source=openai), Jan 2026).

## 2. Price Movements and Supply Outlook
Brent crude prices continued a notable downward trend, with the December 2025 average falling to $63 per barrel—an $11 YoY decrease. This decline is chiefly attributed to persistent production growth (even after reductions), coupled with expanding inventories, which have reached four-year highs. Overall sentiment is bearish, with projections for 2026–2027 suggesting further pressure: the EIA and industry analysts see Brent testing the $56–62 range across 2026 ([IEA Oil Market Report, Dec 2025](https://www.iea.org/reports/oil-market-report-december-2025?utm_source=openai); [Tradingnews.com](https://www.tradingnews.com/news/oil-price-forecast-can-brent-hold-60-usd?utm_source=openai)).

## 3. Analyst Forecasts and Market Sentiment
A new Reuters economist poll and revisions by major banks (JPMorgan, UBS) show a clear downward adjustment in Brent forecasts, reflecting mounting fears of a global surplus and subdued demand. Consensus estimates expect Brent to average $62.23 for 2026, with further risk of slippage. Market participants are discounting concerns related to past geopolitical risks, given the supply overhang ([Reuters cited in Tradingnews.com](https://www.tradingnews.com/news/oil-price-forecast-can-brent-hold-60-usd?utm_source=openai)).

## 4. Global Inventories and Demand/Supply
While OPEC+ production cuts have brought some supply cuts—610,000 bpd drop in November 2025—global inventories surged to four-year highs in October. This disconnect signals that, despite headline production cuts from OPEC+ (especially Russia and Venezuela), demand growth is not keeping pace, intensifying the bearish tilt in the Brent market ([IEA, Dec 2025](https://www.iea.org/reports/oil-market-report-december-2025?utm_source=openai)).

## 5. Geopolitical Considerations
Geopolitical risk premiums have moderated since the peak of Iran’s threats to close the Strait of Hormuz (mid-2025), which fueled brief price spikes and market nervousness. The risk remains in the background, but its effect has diminished as supply-side surpluses and soft demand currently dominate the narrative ([Wikipedia – Iran Threat, Jun 2025](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Iran_threat_of_Strait_of_Hormuz_closure?utm_source=openai)).
